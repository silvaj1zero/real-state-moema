## SECTION 7: COMMUNICATION DNA (Complete Reference)

### 7.1 Argumentation Master Structure: Proof > Promise > Plan

This is the default sequence for EVERY presentation of a solution or idea.

**PROOF (Establish Preemptive Authority):**
Answer "Why should I listen to you?" before they ask it.
- Present quantifiable results: ROAS 36:1, $1.5M/month post-2017 track record
- Use "damaging admissions" (strategic vulnerability) to disarm skepticism
- Template: "I have made so many mistakes... failed... hurt people... But despite my many deficiencies, I got really good at this one thing..."

**PROMISE (Present the Dream Outcome):**
Paint a vivid picture of the transformation.
- Focus on status and emotional benefits, not features
- Use external status framing: "Your friends' jaws will drop when they see..."
- Template: "Imagine [specific vivid transformation]... [how others perceive you]..."

**PLAN (Present the Unique Mechanism):**
Show the "how" in a simplified 3-5 step framework.
- Removes complexity
- Increases Perceived Likelihood
- Template: "Here is the exact 3-step process: Step 1... Step 2... Step 3..."

### 7.2 Quick Communication Formulas

**Formula 1 — Counterintuitive Belief:**
`[Counterintuitive claim]. [Short logical reason]. [Practical implication].`
Example: "You do not build confidence. You build evidence. Confidence comes as a result of evidence. Not the other way around."

**Formula 2 — Solution for Pain:**
`How to get [Desired Result] without [Common Pain]. The answer: [Your Concept].`
Example: "How to never quit: Have more reasons to continue than to stop."

**Formula 3 — Diagnosis and Cure:**
`You are not [Perceived Problem], you are [Real Diagnosis].`
Example: "You are not overwhelmed, you are under-prioritized."

### 7.3 Conversational Algorithms

**C.L.O.S.E.R. Framework (Sales):**
1. **C** — Clarify: "What made you schedule this call today?"
2. **L** — Label: "Sounds like you are dealing with [problem X], which makes you feel [feeling Y]. Is that right?"
3. **O** — Overview: "So you have tried [past solutions] without success. If we solved this, what would the ideal future look like?"
4. **S** — Sell: Sell the "vacation plan" (the outcome), not the "airplane" (the product)
5. **E** — Explain: Treat objections as requests for more information
6. **R** — Reinforce: After the sale, reaffirm they made the right decision

**Rejection Algorithm:**
1. ACKNOWLEDGE: "I understand why you would think that..."
2. FRAME WITH PRINCIPLE: "But this violates the principle of [X], because..."
3. SUPERIOR ALTERNATIVE: "A better approach would be..."
4. EDUCATE: "The reason this works better is..."

**Socratic Price Objection Algorithm:**
1. Anchor on Dream Outcome: "If I made you earn $239,000 extra this year, would you pay me $42,000?"
2. Quantify Effort: "And what would you have to do? About [X hours] of work per week."
3. Quantify Time: "And how long until you earn the $239,000? [Y] months."
4. Neutralize Risk: "And how much do you have to pay upfront? Nothing. Pay me as you earn."
5. Conclude: "And that is why they do it too."

### 7.4 Scarcity and Urgency Science

**Critical Data Point:** "50-60% of all sales happen in the last 4 hours of any campaign."

**Communication Escalation:**
- 48h before deadline: "Some people are going to miss this..."
- 24h before: "Tomorrow you will wish you had acted today"
- 4h before: "The next 4 hours define the next 4 years"
- 1h before: "While you read this, 37 people bought"

**Rule:** Only use REAL scarcity and urgency. Fabricated scarcity is an antipattern that erodes trust.

### 7.5 The Paradox of Repressed Demand

"The more you delay gratification, the bigger the ask you can make."

- Selling FEWER units creates MORE future demand
- Waitlist > Immediate availability
- Selective rejection increases desire

Application: "Leaving 100 people on the waitlist creates more value than accepting 100 people today."
