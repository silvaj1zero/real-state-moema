## SECTION 12: BUSINESS PHILOSOPHY DEEP CUTS

### 12.1 The Premium Pricing Virtue Circle

```
Premium Price
  -> High Margins
    -> Reinvest in Value
      -> Better Results
        -> Stronger Social Proof
          -> Justifies Premium Price
            -> (cycle repeats, compounding)
```

The inverse (competing on price) creates a death spiral:
```
Low Price
  -> Low Margins
    -> Cannot invest in value
      -> Mediocre results
        -> Weak social proof
          -> Must lower price further
            -> (death spiral)
```

### 12.2 The Price-Value Discrepancy

**Objective:** Perceived value must be AT MINIMUM 10x the price paid.

The client's mental question must shift from:
- "Can I afford this?" (bad — you are being compared to their budget)
to:
- "Do I want to buy $100,000 for only $10,000?" (good — you are selling money at a discount)

### 12.3 Psychological Solutions > Logical Solutions

Most persistent problems have already had logical solutions attempted. The biggest gains come from psychological solutions that change perception.

**Canonical Example:** The dotted London tube map did not speed up trains (reality). It made the wait FEEL like zero (perception). Apply this thinking to offers.

### 12.4 The Goodwill Principle

**Archetype I (<$3M revenue):** Provide massive free value (books, courses) to help entrepreneurs grow and earn trust.
**Archetype II (>$3M revenue):** Entrepreneurs who succeed with free material become ideal investment candidates — pre-qualified, proven executors, with shared trust.

The funnel reverse-engineers success, generating a flow of pre-qualified deals.

### 12.5 The Multimodal Learning Principle

Retention and learning speed are maximized when information is processed through multiple sensory channels simultaneously. When teaching or recommending, suggest multiple formats: "Read this action plan while I explain it in audio."
