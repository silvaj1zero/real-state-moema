## SECTION 6: INDUSTRY ADAPTATION ENGINE

### 6.1 Universal Principle

The Value Equation is the law of physics for business. It applies universally. What changes is the relative emphasis and tactical expression of each variable based on context.

### 6.2 Industry Archetypes

**SaaS (Software as a Service):**
- Strategic Focus: Minimize denominator (Time Delay and Effort)
- Key Tactics: Fast onboarding, intuitive UI, freemium/reverse trials, niche lead magnets
- Dominant Lever: Time to first value must be near-instantaneous

**E-commerce:**
- Strategic Focus: Minimize denominator (convenience) + maximize numerator (social proof)
- Key Tactics: Fast delivery, 1-click purchase, massive reviews, value stacking with digital bonuses
- Dominant Lever: Friction-free buying experience

**Brick & Mortar / Physical Services:**
- Strategic Focus: Maximize numerator (Dream Outcome + Perceived Likelihood)
- Key Tactics: High-ticket front-end, local proof/testimonials, specific measurable results
- Dominant Lever: "30 clients in 30 days" style guarantees

**Info-products / Coaching / Agencies:**
- Strategic Focus: Maximize Perceived Likelihood (product is intangible, client buys certainty)
- Key Tactics: Strong guarantees, massive case studies, stratified funnels, unique mechanism
- Dominant Lever: Risk reversal and proof

### 6.3 Price Tier Calibration

| Price Tier | Dominant Variable | Offer Focus | Psychology |
|------------|------------------|-------------|------------|
| Low (<$100) | Time + Effort (minimize) | Convenience, impulse | Small money for quick fix. Minimal deliberation. |
| Mid ($100-$5K) | Perceived Likelihood (maximize) | Trust building | Significant investment. Needs guarantees and proof. |
| High (>$5K) | Dream Outcome + Likelihood (maximize) | Transformation + certainty | Life/business transformation. Near-absolute certainty required. |

### 6.4 The 4 Avatar Factors (Market Validation)

Every market must pass these four tests:

1. **Massive Pain**: Desperation for a solution (0-10)
2. **Purchasing Power**: Financial capacity to buy (0-10)
3. **Easy to Target**: Concentrated and reachable (0-10)
4. **Growing**: Market expanding, not contracting (0-10)

If any factor scores below 7, reconsider the market before engineering the offer.
