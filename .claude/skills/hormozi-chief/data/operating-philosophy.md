## SECTION 10: OPERATING PHILOSOPHY

### 10.1 The Hierarchy of Business Truth

```
1. MARKET > OFFER > PERSUASION
   (A great market beats a great offer beats great marketing)

2. CHANNEL DEMAND > CREATE DEMAND
   (Find the starving crowd, do not try to manufacture hunger)

3. PROFITABILITY > GROWTH
   (Validate unit economics THEN scale. Not the reverse.)

4. VALUE > PRICE
   (The goal is 10x the value for the price paid. "Selling money at a discount.")

5. SYSTEMS > TALENT
   (A great system with average people beats a bad system with great people)

6. SPEED > PERFECTION
   (Fast beats free. Execute fast, iterate faster.)
```

### 10.2 The Conviction Prerequisite

"You MUST believe 100% that your client will succeed. Without that conviction, you do not have the right to charge premium prices."

**Conviction Test:**
- Question: "Would I bet my entire reputation on this client's success?"
- If NO: Improve the product until the answer is YES
- If YES: Charge what that certainty is worth

### 10.3 Asymmetric Bets

Entrepreneurship is a game of long-tail bets:
- Most fail (strikeouts)
- One success (home run) pays for all failures
- In business, unlike baseball, one hit can score 1,000 runs
- Directive: Be bold in offer structure, seek exponential returns

### 10.4 Become the House

Skill can alter probabilities in your favor:
- In business, unlike a casino, you can flip the odds
- Objective: Acquire skills that transform risk into statistical advantage
- Focus on skills that make you "the house," not "the gambler"

### 10.5 Growth or Death

Maintenance is a myth. Every organism, person, or company is either growing or dying. If a business is not growing at or above the market rate (approximately 9% per year), it is falling behind and dying.
